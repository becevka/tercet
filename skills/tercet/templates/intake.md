# Tercet — Intake

Answer what you can. Leave blank what you don't know — the blanks are
valuable information. They tell the Constructor where to search and
the Examiner where to probe.

This intake becomes the seed of your Living Brief. The Constructor will
enrich it with evidence from available tools. The Examiner will challenge
what looks weak. What neither can resolve comes back to you.

---

## Intent

What do you want to build, change, or decide? And why now — what triggered this?

<!-- Both the "what" and the "why now" matter. A feature request triggered by
     a customer escalation is a different animal than the same feature on a
     roadmap. The trigger shapes the constraints. -->


## Known Context

What do you already know about the systems, people, or situation involved?

<!-- Services, tools, teams, processes, dependencies — whatever's relevant.
     Don't worry about being exhaustive. The Constructor will search for
     more. Just give it a starting point. -->


## What You Know That Isn't Written Down

**This is the most important section.**

Things like:
- "Service X retries silently and you won't see failures in the logs"
- "Team Y owns this but they're understaffed and will push back"
- "This worked differently before the 2023 migration and some old behavior still leaks through"
- "The on-call engineer manually restarts this every Tuesday"
- "The VP says this is a priority but last quarter's priority got quietly shelved"
- "Two directors disagree about the approach and neither will say so in a meeting"

This is the knowledge that no tool can retrieve. Don't filter yourself.


## What Are You Worried About?

Hunches. Gut feelings. Political landmines. Things that have gone wrong
before. Half-formed concerns you can't fully articulate.

<!-- These often point directly to where the real risk lives. The Examiner
     will turn your hunches into specific questions. -->


## Constraints

What's non-negotiable? Time, budget, team size, technical limitations,
political boundaries, compliance requirements.

<!-- Distinguish between hard constraints ("must ship before audit") and
     soft ones ("leadership prefers we use the existing platform"). The
     Examiner will question whether soft constraints are actually hard. -->


## What Documentation Exists?

Links or paths to anything relevant: architecture docs, runbooks, service
maps, incident postmortems, Jira epics, Slack threads, past proposals.

<!-- The Constructor will use these as starting points for its research.
     The Examiner will question whether they're current. -->
